# Wi-Fi Planning — Channel & Band Strategy

## Frequency Band Overview

| Band                | Non-Overlapping Channels | Characteristics                  | Use Case                    |
| ------------------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| 📶 2.4 GHz          | 1, 6, 11                 | Better range, lower bandwidth    | IoT / low bandwidth devices |
| ⚡ 5 GHz             | 36–48, 149–161           | High speed, shorter range        | Office / Staff connectivity |
| 🚀 6 GHz (Wi-Fi 6E) | PSC channels             | Ultra-high speed, low congestion | High-density environments   |

# Step-by-Step AP Deployment (Ubiquiti UniFi Example)

## Step 1: Install UniFi Controller

📦 Install UniFi Network Controller:

* Download from Ubiquiti
* Install on:

  * Server **OR**
  * Cloud VM

---

## Step 2: AP Placement

📍 Mount access points:

* Height: **2.5 – 3 meters**
* Position: Centralized coverage zones
* Avoid:

  * Walls
  * Metal columns
  * Microwave ovens

---

## Step 3: Network Connection

🔌 Connect APs to PoE switches:

* Port VLAN: **VLAN 10 (Management)**
* AP management traffic is tagged to VLAN 10

---

## Step 4: Adopt Access Points

📍 In UniFi Controller:

* Navigate to **Devices**
* APs appear as **Pending**
* Click **Adopt** for each AP

---

## Step 5: Create Wireless Networks

📶 Navigate to:

`Settings > Wireless Networks > Add`

### Configure SSIDs:

* Corp-Staff:

  * Security: WPA2/WPA3 Enterprise
  * VLAN: 20

* Corp-Guest:

  * Security: WPA2-PSK
  * VLAN: 30

---

## Step 6: RF Planning

📡 Navigate to:

`Settings > RF Environment`

### Actions:

* Run channel scan per AP
* Assign least congested channels

---

## Step 7: TX Power Configuration

⚡ Set transmit power:

* Mode: **Auto or Medium**

⚠️ High TX power may cause:

* Poor roaming behavior
* Clients sticking to distant APs

---

## Step 8: Enable Band Steering

🔀 Feature: Band Steering

* Automatically pushes dual-band clients to 5 GHz
* Improves performance and reduces 2.4 GHz congestion

---

## Step 9: Enable Fast Roaming

🚶 Feature: 802.11r Fast Roaming

* Enables seamless AP handoff
* Improves mobility experience for users

---

## Step 10: Configure WPA2-Enterprise (Corp-Staff)

🔐 Configure RADIUS authentication:

| Parameter        | Value                   |
| ---------------- | ----------------------- |
| RADIUS Server IP | Windows AD / NPS Server |
| Port             | 1812                    |
| Shared Secret    | Defined secure key      |

---

## Step 11: Testing & Validation

🧪 Perform connectivity tests:

* Connect device to each SSID
* Verify correct VLAN assignment
* Confirm IP address subnet
* Validate internet access
* Ensure no cross-VLAN access

---

## Step 12: Coverage Survey

📊 Perform RF validation:

* Tools:

  * Ekahau
  * UniFi built-in heatmap

### Objectives:

* Identify dead zones
* Adjust AP placement
* Optimize signal coverage

---

# Best Practice Tips

## 📶 AP Coverage Design

* Ensure **15–20% overlap** between adjacent AP coverage areas
* Enables smooth roaming without disconnects

---

## 🔑 Guest Wi-Fi Security

* Rotate Guest PSK regularly (daily or weekly)
* Prevents long-term password sharing

---

## 🛡️ WPA3 Security Recommendation

* Use WPA3 wherever supported
* Benefits:

  * Forward secrecy
  * Protection of past sessions even if key is compromised

---

## 🚫 Network Isolation Rule

* Never bridge Guest SSID directly to LAN
* Always route Guest traffic through:

  * Dedicated VLAN
  * Firewall security zone

---

## 📉 Legacy Protocol Removal

* Disable legacy 802.11b rates:

  * 1 Mbps
  * 2 Mbps
  * 5.5 Mbps
  * 11 Mbps

⚠️ Reason:

* These rates degrade overall wireless performance

---

## 📡 RF Interference Prevention

Avoid AP placement near:

* Metal ceilings
* HVAC ducting
* Fluorescent lighting systems

These can cause:

* Signal degradation
* Packet loss
* Unstable connections
